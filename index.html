Hedy Lamarr

Actress by day and inventor by night

Here's a time line of Hedy Lamarr's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Vienna, Austria-Hungary
  • 1933 - Played a role in the controvertial movie Ecstasy
  • 1938 - Moved to the USA and entered Hollywood
  • 1940 - Invented frequency-hopping system which lays foundation to modern technologies such as GPS, WIFI
  • 1997 - Recognised by the public
  • 2000 - Died at the age of 95
"The world isn't getting any easier. With all these new inventions I believe that people are hurried more and pushed more... The hurried way is not the right way; you need time for everything - time to work, time to play, time to rest." -- Hedy Lamarr
